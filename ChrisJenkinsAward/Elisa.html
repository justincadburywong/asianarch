Dr. Tong is a leader in cancer prevention and control among Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders, particularly in the area of tobacco control. She served as the Research Director for the Sacramento site of the Asian American Cancer Research Awareness, Research, and Training (AANCART) and launched the Sacramento Asian Pacific Islander Cancer Caucus. Dr. Tong has exposed the tobacco industry’s efforts that undermined linking secondhand smoke with cancer and other diseases, in efforts to prevent global smoke-free policies. She has described smoking behavior among Asian Americans in California and showed that smoke-free policies are particularly effective for Asian American smokers' cessation and nonsmokers' protection. She has worked in partnership with San Francisco Chinatown Public Health Center and Asian Pacific Partners for Empowerment Advocacy and Leadership on AANHPI community-based tobacco research projects. She has also worked on international tobacco control projects in China. Dr. Tong initiated UC Quits, the UC-wide Tobacco Cessation Network, and launched CA Quits, a statewide tobacco cessation learning collaborative. She also led statewide outreach to increase calls by Medi- Cal members who smoke to the California Smokers' Helpline, which offers Asian language lines. She has received the UC Davis School of Medicine Dean’s Team Excellence Award, the American Legacy Foundation Sybil G. Jacobs Award for Outstanding Use of Tobacco Industry Documents, and the American Public Health Association Asian Pacific Islander Caucus' "Best Published Paper" Award.
