Dr. McPhee is a pioneer in the fields of Asian American health research and community-based participatory research. Together with Christopher Jenkins, he established one of the first community-academic research units on Asian American health, the Vietnamese Community Health Promotion Project (Suc Khoe La Vang!) at UCSF in 1986. Dr. McPhee conducted the first community-wide intervention trials and randomized controlled trials of health promotion interventions such as mass media and lay health workers outreach among Vietnamese Americans on topics such as tobacco control, breast and cervical cancer screening, colorectal cancer screening, as well as hepatitis B vaccination and screening. He was the San Francisco Regional Principal Investigator of the Asian American Cancer Research Awareness, Research, and Training (AANCART) from 2000 to 2008. In his career, Dr. McPhee obtained nearly 50 grants and over $36 million in research funding and published more than 80 research articles on Asian American health.
