Community-Engaged Research Partnership 101Training

The Health Research Partnerships in Asian Communities Project provided the Community-Engaged Research Partnership 101 trainings to improve knowledge on Community-Based Participatory Research (CBPR).

The learning objectives were by the end of the training, the participants would be able to:

  • Identify the spectrum, principles, benefits, and challenges of CBPR
  • Identify the principles, common issues, and benefits of and barriers to partnerships
  • Identify the qualities of a partner and a healthy partnership

The project was supported by Award Number RC4MD005709 from the National Institutes of Health, Office of the Director and the National Center on Minority Health and Health Disparities. The Health Research Partnerships in Asian Communities Project is a collaboration between the Cancer Prevention Institute of California, the University of California, San Francisco, and the Vietnamese Reach for Health Coalition.

Strengthening Partnerships and Defining Research Strategies Training

The Health Research Partnerships in Asian Communities Project provided the Strengthening Partnerships and Defining Research Strategies training to improve knowledge and skills to develop and strengthen community-academic partnerships and define their research strategies. 

The learning objectives were by the end of the training, the participants would be able to:

  • Assess the stage of their partnership
  • Assess needs and assets of each partner
  • Identify the purpose, components, and common mechanisms of partnership agreements
  • Draft a partnership agreement
  • Select a research topic and practice writing research questions
  • Identify some common funding agencies and grant mechanisms
  • Identify the four main sections of a National Institutes of Health grant proposal
  • Identify issues of protecting human participants and Institutional Review Board

The project was supported by Award Number RC4MD005709 from the National Institutes of Health, Office of the Director and the National Center on Minority Health and Health Disparities. Proposal Writing Workshop

The Health Research Partnerships in Asian Communities Project provided the Proposal Writing workshop to improve skills to write National Institutes of Health (NIH) style proposals.

The learning objectives were by the end of the workshop the participants would be able to:

  • Assess the required elements of a proposal from reviewing an NIH Funding Opportunity Announcement
  • Identify the criteria that will be used to evaluate the proposal
  • Identify the audience of the proposal
  • Identify what reviewers look for while they read a proposal
  • Identify the style of writing in a proposal
  • Practice drafting a section of the proposal
